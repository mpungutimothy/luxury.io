The 2016 Nissan Maxima is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine with 300 horsepower, 10 more than the engine in the outgoing model. A continuously variable transmission and front-wheel drive are standard in all models. Nissan expects the 2016 Maxima to return 22/30 mpg city/highway, which is an improvement over the previous model's EPA-estimated 19/26 mpg.

The 2016 Nissan Maxima seats five and comes with a power-adjustable driver,seat, an eight-speaker audio system, Bluetooth, satellite radio, HD Radio, push-button start, a rearview camera, two USB ports, the NissanConnect infotainment system, navigation, an 8-inch color display screen and voice controls for phone, audio and navigation functions. Leather upholstery, heated and ventilated front seats, an 11-speaker Bose audio system, a 360-degree parking camera system, adaptive cruise control, blind spot warning, rear cross traffic alert, front and rear parking sensors and forward collision warning with automatic braking are available. The 2016 Nissan Maxima starts at $33,235 including destination fees.

The 2016 Nissan Maxima is an absolute joy to test drive. From the moment you step inside, you'll feel cocooned in a world of luxury and comfort. The leather-appointed seats are plush and supportive, ensuring that even on long drives, you won't experience any discomfort. As you grip the steering wheel, the smoothness of the ride becomes apparent. The V6 engine provides ample power, effortlessly propelling you forward with each press of the accelerator.

The CVT transmission seamlessly shifts through gears, offering a responsive and fuel-efficient driving experience. Whether navigating city streets or hitting the open highway, the Maxima handles with confidence and precision, thanks to its sport-tuned suspension and responsive steering.

The handling was precise and responsive, allowing me to confidently maneuver through curves and tight turns. The advanced safety features such as forward collision warning and blind-spot monitoring added an extra layer of reassurance during my drive. Overall, the 2016 Nissan Maxima delivers not only on style but also on performance and comfort, making it an excellent choice for those seeking a stylish sedan with a sporty edge.
